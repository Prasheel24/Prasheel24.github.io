The Omnidirectional Mobile Robot - Ballbot

  • Prasheel Renkuntla, Shubham Sonawane
  • MATLAB, SIMULINK

The ballbot is an omnidirectional balancing mobile robot. This project is the analysis of the original paper published by Dr. Nagarajan (CMU) on the Ballbot - Link.

source: google.com

This project discusses the author's approach to the design and development of the dynamic model and control architecture of the robot. Balancing Control and Station Keeping Control are two main controllers that can be simulated without a real robot.

Balancing Control on Body Angle

These controllers are designed in MATLAB. The Balancing control is a PID controller applied on the State Space representation of the system. The Stationkeeping Control is designed on top of this control with an external PD control to hold the robot steady at a given point. The results are verified with simulation in MATLAB and SIMULINK.

Station Keeping Control output

Dependencies: MATLAB

This project was developed as a part of the Control of Robotic Systems course. Thus, designed and simulated the nested control architecture of the ballbot in MATLAB and SIMULINK, to stabilize the robot with an exceptional station keeping (PID and PD) control that balanced the robot in under 10 seconds.
